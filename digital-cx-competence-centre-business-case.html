What we mean by "Digital Experience Platform"

A DXP is not a single piece of software. It is the combination of platforms (CMS, CRM, analytics, search), the files and assets that populate them (content, media, metadata, data), the processes that keep them running (governance, workflows, publishing, optimisation), and the expertise that ties it all together. Our Competence Centre delivers across all four layers — that integrated combination is what we sell, and what no single in-house hire can replicate.

The Problem

What our clients are struggling with

Four pain points that open the door to our Competence Centre model.

€75–170K

The FTE cost trap

Hiring a single digital specialist (Content, SEO, Data) in Germany costs €48–85K in salary alone. Add social contributions (~22%), benefits, tools, onboarding and management overhead — the true cost is 1.5–2× salary. For a team of 3, that's €225–510K per year.

3–6mo

Hiring is slow and unreliable

The average time to hire a digital specialist in Germany is 3–6 months. Talent attrition is high. Senior expertise in SXO, GEO and data analytics is scarce and expensive — companies are left with capability gaps at critical moments.

10+

Multi-market complexity

Global brands need localised digital content across 10+ markets — each with its own language, CMS instance, SEO strategy, and regulatory nuance. Managing this with local hires creates inconsistency, duplication and cost spirals.

Siloed

Disconnected capability

Content, SEO, UX, analytics and CMS are managed in silos — different agencies, different tools, no unified strategy. The customer journey is broken at the seams. SXO (Search + UX + Content) demands integration nobody currently provides cleanly.

The critical insight: companies want expert capability on demand — not permanent headcount. They want the output of a specialist team without the overhead, risk and rigidity of employment. That is exactly what our model delivers.

Threats
